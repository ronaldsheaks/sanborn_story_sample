Institutions were not just venues. They were infrastructure.

This application turns the dataset into a public-facing tour: anchor institutions, corridor institutions, relationship lines, archival media targets, and governance indicators in one narrative interface.

Transparent workflow

From archive to public map

The model helps prioritize sources, but the publication layer remains defensible: every claim can point back to source pages, confidence notes, and human verification.

1. CorpusSanborn maps, directories, papers, oral histories, media pages.
2. OCR / ingestionTurn unstructured PDFs, scans, and transcripts into searchable text.
3. NER extractionPull names, places, organizations, venues, streets, and dates.
4. GIS QANormalize addresses, geocode, flag uncertainty, and verify with maps.
5. Governance codingMark labor coordination, mentorship, civic assembly, continuity, and participation.
6. Story mapPublish narrative, points, media, timelines, and source trails.
